# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Ceird serves construction and trades teams who need one web workspace to handle both office and site work. Primary users include tradespeople, site managers, contractors, subcontractors, builders, and office admins. The same product must support dense desktop workflows in the office and fast, reliable mobile use on site, with role-gated surfaces rather than separate apps.

Users are coordinating jobs, sites, labels, locations, teams, activity, organization settings, and contractor/subcontractor relationships. They need confidence that the workspace reflects the right organization, role, customer, site, and job context before they act.

## Product Purpose

Ceird is a Linear-style product workspace for trades and construction teams. It gives each contractor, subcontractor, builder, tenant, or related organization a dedicated authenticated workspace while allowing work to become interlinked across organizations where the real-world relationship demands it.

The product exists to replace scattered messages, spreadsheets, generic project-management flows, and office-only tools with a focused operating layer for jobs and sites. AI should become part of the working surface: assisting with organization, triage, search, summaries, and next actions without making the interface feel generated or ornamental.

Success looks like a small or medium construction business running its active work from Ceird because the product is faster, clearer, more interactive, and more trustworthy than the workflows they used before.

## Brand Personality

Functional, interactive, Linear-like, delightful.

The brand should feel precise, confident, modern, tool-native, and quietly delightful. It should respect tradespeople and construction admins as capable professionals who deserve best-in-class software, not simplified or patronizing software. The product should feel as polished as Linear, as composed as Vercel, as cleanly operational as Midday AI, and compatible with the best shadcn UI dashboard examples.

Delight in Ceird should come from speed, exact feedback, satisfying state changes, thoughtful defaults, and small moments that make the system feel alive and confident. Delight should never make serious work feel unserious.

## Anti-references

Do not make Ceird look like generic project-management SaaS. Avoid macho construction branding, high-vis cliches, safety-poster energy, bubbly startup UI, dashboard clutter, and anything that reads as obviously AI-generated.

Do not confuse delight with cartoon personality, confetti-cannon celebration, joke copy, mascot energy, or theatrical motion. Ceird can be warm, satisfying, and memorable, but it should stay composed and operational.

Avoid decorative AI aesthetics: purple glow, glassy panels, vague assistant magic, and synthetic illustration-first interfaces. AI should feel embedded in expert workflows, not sprayed on top.

Avoid reducing the product to a basic field app. Site users need intuition, speed, and resilience, but they do not need the interface to be plain, dull, or visually underpowered.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn Linear-level trust.** Every component should feel familiar, exact, and coherent enough that power users can move quickly without second-guessing the interface.
2. **Design one workspace for two contexts.** Office admins and site users share the same product, so layouts must adapt structurally across desktop and mobile while preserving the same mental model.
3. **Make organization context impossible to miss.** Multi-tenant and inter-organization workflows must always make the active workspace, role, job, site, and permissions legible.
4. **Let AI act like infrastructure.** AI features should reduce coordination work, expose useful intent, and accelerate decisions without becoming the visual identity.
5. **Prefer dense clarity over decorative simplicity.** The product can be rich and highly interactive, but every control, label, view, and animation must serve the work.
6. **Make the product delightful through precision.** Crisp transitions, responsive controls, completion states, tactile pressed states, and useful microcopy should make the interface feel cared for without turning work into performance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Ceird should meet a high product-quality bar for keyboard access, focus management, semantic structure, responsive behavior, and legible interaction states. Command-K, command bars, route hotkeys, discoverable shortcuts, and focused keyboard workflows are part of the product identity.

Accessibility should strengthen craft rather than flatten it. Use expressive status, label, and map visuals, but pair important meaning with text, icons, position, or interaction affordances where needed. Respect user motion preferences for nonessential animation while preserving polished state transitions. Mobile and touch ergonomics matter because the same app must work on site, but site use is not a reason to make the interface simplistic.
